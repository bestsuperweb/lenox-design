9. Phishing.

Phishing is a scam where identity thieves use fraudulent emails, phony Web sites, online pop-ups, and even phone calls in an attempt to trick you into providing personal information such as bank account numbers, credit card numbers, passwords and social security numbers. Olnick will never ask you to e-mail personal information such as passwords, social security number, bank account details, credit card numbers, or other financial information nor will Olnick make unsolicited calls asking for such information. However, for fraud prevention purposes, Olnick may ask for you to verify personal information such as address or telephone number, which you have previously provided. Olnick will never send you e-mails or call you claiming we have lost or are updating your records. If you receive a suspicious message (e.g., email, text message, SMS, voicemail), especially from a source claiming to be Olnick or related to Olnick, do not provide the information requested and notify Olnick by either forwarding the suspicious message or sending an email with as much detail as possible of the content and sender of the suspicious message to info@olnick.com.
